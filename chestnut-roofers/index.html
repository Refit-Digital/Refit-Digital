Frequently Asked Questions

Got a question about roofing in Liverpool? We've answered the most common ones below.

A new roof in Liverpool typically costs between £5,000 and £12,000 depending on the size of your property, the materials chosen, and the complexity of the job. Every property is different, which is why Chestnut Roofers and Builders offer free, no-obligation surveys — call us on 0151 733 0170 for an accurate quote.

In most cases, a like-for-like roof replacement falls under permitted development and doesn't require planning permission. However, if you're changing the roof shape, height, or converting a flat roof to pitched, you may need approval from Liverpool City Council. We can advise on this during your free survey.

A typical semi-detached house in Liverpool takes around 3 to 5 working days for a full re-roof, weather permitting. Larger properties or more complex work like flat-to-pitched conversions may take a week or more. We always aim to keep disruption to a minimum and leave the site tidy each day.

Yes — flat-to-pitched conversions are one of our specialities. A pitched roof gives you better drainage, improved insulation, and a much longer lifespan. Our team have completed many successful conversions across Liverpool, and the results speak for themselves — just check our Google reviews.

Dry ridge systems use mechanical fixings instead of sand and cement mortar. They're far more durable, weather-resistant, and effectively maintenance-free — mortar ridges crack and crumble over time. We fit dry ridge tiles as standard on all our roofing work.

Key warning signs include missing or slipped slates, cracked tiles, damp patches on your ceilings, daylight visible through the roof space, or sagging ridges. Isolated damage can often be repaired, but widespread issues usually mean a replacement is more cost-effective in the long run. We offer free roof surveys across Liverpool.

Absolutely. Chestnut Roofers and Builders offer completely free, no-obligation quotes for all roofing and building work across Liverpool and Merseyside. We'll come out, inspect your roof, explain exactly what's needed, and give you a clear, honest price — no pressure.

Aluminium guttering typically lasts 30+ years compared to 15–20 for uPVC. It's stronger, doesn't warp in sunlight, and looks smarter on most properties. We fit aluminium as our preferred option, though we also install uPVC if you'd prefer a lower-cost alternative.

Yes — Chestnut Roofers and Builders are fully insured for all roofing and building work. We've been trading in Liverpool since 1976, which means nearly 50 years of hands-on experience. Contact us for details of our insurance cover and specific accreditations.

We recommend a professional inspection at least every two to three years, or after any severe storms. Regular checks catch small problems — a slipped slate, a blocked gutter — before they escalate into costly repairs. Chestnut Roofers offer free roof inspections across Liverpool and Merseyside.
